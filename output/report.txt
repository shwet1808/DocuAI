# Executive Summary
The National Testing Agency (NTA) has released the official notification for the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for Undergraduate programs (NEET UG) 2026. The exam is the single, mandatory entrance test for admission to undergraduate medical (MBBS), dental (BDS), and allied health science courses across India. A significant development for the 2026 cycle is that the initial exam was cancelled, and a re-examination is scheduled for **June 21, 2026**. The application window has closed, and a correction window was available from March 10-12, 2026. Candidates must meet specific eligibility criteria regarding age, educational qualification, and subjects studied.

# Detailed Analysis

**1. Exam Overview & Important Dates:**
*   **Exam Name:** NEET (UG) – 2026
*   **Conducting Body:** National Testing Agency (NTA)
*   **Exam Status:** The originally scheduled 2026 exam was cancelled. A **re-examination is scheduled for June 21, 2026**.
*   **Official Website:** [https://neet.nta.nic.in/](https://neet.nta.nic.in/)
*   **Application Timeline:** The primary application window is closed. The last date for fee payment was **March 8, 2026**.
*   **Correction Window:** A facility to edit specific details in the submitted application form was open from **March 10 to March 12, 2026**.
*   **Admit Card:** To be released on the official website ahead of the re-exam date.

**2. Eligibility Criteria:**
A candidate must fulfill all the following criteria to be eligible for NEET UG 2026:
*   **Age Limit:**
    *   **Minimum Age:** Must be 17 years of age as of December 31, 2026.
    *   **Maximum Age:** There is **NO upper age limit** for appearing in NEET UG as per current guidelines.
*   **Educational Qualification:**
    *   Must have passed Class 12 or equivalent examination.
    *   Must have studied **Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English** in Class 12.
*   **Minimum Qualifying Marks:** The aggregate marks required in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology in the qualifying examination are:
    *   **General / EWS:** 50%
    *   **OBC / SC / ST:** 40%
    *   **PwD (General):** 45%
    *   **PwD (OBC/SC/ST):** 40%
*   **Number of Attempts:** There is **NO limit on the number of attempts** for NEET UG.

**3. Exam Pattern & Syllabus:**
*   **Mode:** Offline (Pen and Paper-based OMR sheet).
*   **Duration:** 3 hours and 20 minutes.
*   **Sections:** Physics, Chemistry, Botany, Zoology.
*   **Number of Questions:** 200 (50 from each section).
*   **Marking Scheme:** +4 for each correct answer, -1 for each incorrect answer.
*   **Syllabus:** Based on the NCERT curriculum of Classes 11 and 12.

**4. Recent Updates & Notices:**
The NTA's official website ([nta.ac.in](https://nta.ac.in)) shows active notices for the 2026 re-examination, including:
*   Preparation for a smooth and secure conduct of the re-exam.
*   Advisories on biometric verification and exception protocols.
*   Notices regarding changes in examination venues for specific centers.
